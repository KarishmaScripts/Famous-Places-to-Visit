Banff National Park, Canada

Banff National Park is Canada's first and one of the most iconic national parks, located in the Canadian Rockies in the province of Alberta. Established in 1885, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a world-renowned destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and those seeking breathtaking mountain landscapes.

Location: Banff National Park is situated in the Canadian Rockies in Alberta, Canada, and covers an area of about 6,641 square kilometers (2,564 square miles).
Proximity to Major Cities: The park is located around 130 kilometers (81 miles) west of Calgary, the closest major city, and just a short drive from the resort town of Banff and Lake Louise.
The park includes majestic mountain peaks, crystal-clear lakes, glaciers, forests, valleys, and hot springs. It is part of the larger Canadian Rocky Mountain Parks World Heritage Site, recognized for its outstanding natural beauty and ecological importance.

Banff is famous for its striking mountain landscapes. The park features towering peaks, deep valleys, rugged cliffs, and impressive glaciers. Some of the most famous mountains and landmarks include:
Mount Rundle: An iconic mountain known for its sharp ridge and distinctive shape.
Cascade Mountain: Located near the town of Banff, it offers spectacular views of the surrounding valley.
Mount Assiniboine: Often called the "Matterhorn of the Rockies," it is one of the most photographed mountains in Canada.
Glaciers: Banff is home to glaciers like the Columbia Icefield, which is the largest icefield in the Rockies.
